## Candidate Overlay Records

### `baseline-establishment.absolute-yardstick-before-contrast`

Why inject: current `baseline-establishment` already handles starting
conditions and comparison standards, but Bevelin adds a crisp pressure that is
especially relevant to contrast-misreaction: remove the adjacent comparison and
ask what yardstick still decides the choice.

Expected effect:

- The private packet should surface an independent threshold when an answer says
  an option is acceptable mainly because it is better than a nearby alternative.
- Step 6, if the chunk is later used, should translate this into a go/no-go,
  revise, or evidence-gathering condition, not a broad comparison checklist.

### `obligations-controls-mapping.role-reversal-system-rule`

Why inject: current `obligations-controls-mapping` turns responsibility into
controls. Bevelin adds a sharper fairness operator: design the rule as a system
that would still be acceptable if roles were reversed.

Expected effect:

- The private packet should move fairness cases from "they should be fair" to
  "what rule, inspection point, or consequence would be fair from either side?"
- Step 6, if the chunk is later used, should produce a boundary or operating
  rule, not moral pressure.
