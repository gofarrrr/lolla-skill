# Reasoning Substrate Packet Fixture Review

**Date:** 2026-05-06
**PR slice:** PR27 - mixed packet fixture review
**Status:** review-only fixture; no extraction, runtime, prompt, lane, model-call, judge, or user-facing surface
**Decision label:** `mixed_packet_fixture_useful`

## Verdict

The mixed `reasoning_substrate_packet.v1` fixture is useful enough to justify
the next controlled enrichment step.

It is not product output and should not be promoted as one. But as a handoff
object for the next LLM/reviewer, it passes the first usefulness check:

- v4-reviewed cards add operational depth the graph alone does not provide;
- source-custodied graph-only cards remain useful shelf hints;
- source custody is visible without pretending to be v4 reviewed depth;
- provenance, coverage labels, blocked surfaces, and suppression are
  inspectable;
- Python packages the material but does not choose the conclusion.

The fixture also shows the main remaining thin spot: graph-only cards are good
for recall and orientation, but they do not yet provide enough operational
requirements, misuse guards, diagnostic questions, or source-backed dismissal
paths for high-confidence treatment.

## Review Answers

### Is The Packet Compact Enough?

Yes, for a review-only fixture.

The first generated version used two snippets per field and felt too heavy for
the "tiny packet" purpose. PR27 reduced the cap to one high-value snippet per
field. At about `34 KB`, the packet is still not tiny in a human-scannable
Markdown sense, but it is compact enough for an LLM handoff and small enough
for reviewer inspection.

The useful compactness pattern is:

- `transaction_context` gives just enough case setup;
- each card has one clear coverage status;
- v4 cards carry one reviewed snippet per affordance field;
- graph-only cards carry one graph item per field;
- suppression is separate from candidate cards;
- blocked surfaces stay explicit.

Next hardening should focus on card ordering and maybe a short per-card
`reviewer_summary` generated deterministically from existing fields only, not
on adding more snippets.

### Are v4-Reviewed Cards Meaningfully Richer?

Yes.

The v4 cards provide things the runtime graph does not:

- operational `case_evidence_needed`;
- `do_not_use_when` dismissal conditions;
- diagnostic questions;
- misuse guards;
- treatment requirements;
- exact source evidence with reviewed source custody;
- absence records that prevent overclaiming.

`opportunity-cost` is the clearest example. The graph can say "name the
displaced alternative." The v4 card adds the treatment discipline: the
alternative must be real, executable, and in the same scarce-resource currency.
That is useful reasoning material, not just a model name.

### Are Graph-Only Cards Still Useful?

Yes, but as lighter shelf hints.

`step-back`, `constraints`, `chain-of-verification`, and `confirmation-bias`
are not empty. Their runtime graph fields give the next LLM a compact reminder
of when to select the model, what can go wrong, how failure appears, and what
premortem question to ask.

They are useful for broad recall and for helping the LLM decide what to
inspect. They are not yet strong enough to make source-backed operational
claims. The packet labels that honestly with `graph_only_runtime_card`, empty
`reviewed_affordance_fields`, and a do-not-overclaim note.

### Does Source Custody Improve Trust Without Pretending To Be v4?

Yes.

PR27 adds explicit `source_custody` metadata to candidate cards:

- `custody_status`
- `source_file`
- `manifest_path`
- `sha256`
- `bytes`
- `v4_reviewed_record_available`
- `v4_reviewed_affordance_available`

For graph-only cards, source custody now says:

> the source is in the repo and can be inspected later,
> but no reviewed v4 record or reviewed v4 affordance is available now.

That is the correct distinction. It makes the card more trustworthy without
turning custody into reviewed depth.

### Is Provenance Clear?

Mostly yes.

The fixture preserves:

- `pulled_by` lane/reviewer provenance;
- `why_pulled` reason;
- evidence source type;
- optional quote;
- route or artifact ID;
- lane order and score when supplied;
- duplicate suppression reason.

The current provenance is good enough for explicit nominations. A future
route-trace adapter will still need a mechanical policy for translating raw
Lane 2 capped/accepted/rejected buckets and Lane 3/Lane 4 candidate routes into
candidate versus suppressed nominations.

### Are Coverage Labels Clear?

Yes.

The packet makes the 222/55/167 distinction visible:

- v4 cards: `v4_reviewed_affordance_available`
- graph-only cards: `graph_only_runtime_card`
- graph-only IDs listed under `missing_reviewed_model_ids`
- source custody separately visible on every card
- suppressed duplicate still carries a coverage status

This prevents v4 from silently becoming "the only models that matter" while
also preventing graph-only cards from masquerading as reviewed affordance
records.

### Useful Reasoning Material Or Internal Clutter?

Useful reasoning material, with one caveat.

The cards are more than internal machinery because the LLM can read them as:

- why this shelf was pulled;
- what evidence class nominated it;
- what the graph says;
- what reviewed v4 adds;
- what source support is missing;
- what not to overclaim;
- which surfaces remain blocked.

The caveat is that raw JSON is still an internal format. The receiving LLM can
use it, and a reviewer can inspect it, but a later human-facing review view
would need a compact rendered table or card diff. That is not PR27 work.

## What Feels Missing Before Extraction

The fixture identifies a real extraction need.

The graph-only cards that feel most worth enriching are:

1. `chain-of-verification`
2. `constraints`
3. `confirmation-bias`
4. `step-back`

Why:

- They are already nominated by existing lane-like provenance in the fixture.
- They appear in PR25's static lane-signal priority set or close to it.
- Their graph fields are useful but too broad for operational treatment.
- They need reviewed source-backed misuse guards, diagnostic questions, and
  dismissal conditions to become as actionable as the v4 cards.

What is missing before a live adapter:

- route-trace-to-nomination mechanical policy;
- per-lane suppression rules that preserve capped/rejected candidates without
  semantic judgment;
- optional fixture comparison across two or three archived transactions;
- a compact reviewer view for packet inspection.

Those are not blockers for a first controlled extraction batch. They are
blockers for live route-trace packet production.
