## Rubric Validation

### Are the 4 levels separable?

YES. The levels cluster around distinct language patterns:

- **0.95** = constitutive, essential, cannot-function-without. The article describes the ally as the primary mechanism, a prerequisite, or near-identity.
- **0.90** = strong, specific synergy. The article describes a named, directional mechanism that makes the source model more effective.
- **0.80** = moderate, complementary. The article describes a useful relationship but with generic language ("strengthens", "helps") or from opposite-direction framing.
- **0.70** = supportive, supplementary. The relationship was extracted from a non-Latticework section (mitigations, strengths) and describes a generic tool.

### Does the same model get different strengths as ally of different models?

YES — critical validation that differentiation is directional, not a property of the target model:

- **second-order-thinking**: 0.95 as ally of premortem ("requires participants to engage in") but 0.80 as ally of inversion ("naturally synergizes")
- **checklists**: 0.70 as ally of premortem (mitigations-section tool) but 0.80 as ally of lock-in ("locks work processes into consistent, repeatable patterns")

### Are there thin-Latticework articles that resist differentiation?

margin-of-safety has only 1 retained ally (probabilistic-thinking). Differentiation is trivially achieved. No article was too thin to produce a meaningful assessment.

### Why is 0.70 so rare?

The curation pipeline already filtered out the weakest relationships. Most retained allies represent at least a moderate synergy. The 0.70 level remains meaningful for allies sourced from mitigations or strengths sections rather than the explicit Latticework ally table.

## Patterns Observed

1. **Cognitive bias models** (confirmation-bias, survivorship-bias) tend to have at least one 0.95 ally — the underlying mechanism or primary antidote.

2. **Operational/strategic models** (power-dynamics, game-theory-payoffs, lock-in) tend to cluster at 0.80-0.90 — useful complements rather than constitutive relationships.

3. **Reasoning discipline models** (inversion, premortem, first-principles-thinking) tend to have one 0.95 ally (their procedural complement) and several 0.80s.

4. **The Latticework section** consistently provides the strongest language. Allies extracted from other sections (Mitigations, Strengths) tend to score 0.70-0.80.

5. **Directionality matters.** The same model scores differently as an ally of different source models, confirming that affinity is a property of the relationship in context, not the target model in isolation.

**Proceed with the remaining 212 articles using the 4-level rubric as-is.**

No adjustment needed. The levels separate cleanly, directional variation appears naturally, and the language markers are consistent enough to scale.
