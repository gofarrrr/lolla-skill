Internal can be the right answer in some scenarios, but this isn't obviously one of them. Here's the filter I'd apply.

Internal is better when: (a) the conduct is isolated and the leadership you're reporting to is genuinely independent from it, (b) the firm has a track record of taking internal reports seriously, (c) the regulator's program is weak or doesn't fit your situation.
