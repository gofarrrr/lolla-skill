# Seeking Wisdom Lane 1 Enrichment Map

Date: 2026-05-11

## Thesis

`Lolla-system-b` is the source lineage: the Munger tendency ontology, the
canonical mental-model corpus, the curated Munger-to-model bridge, and the
first Structural Pressure engine were built there.

`lolla-skill` is the living implementation: conversation-first capture,
IR/provenance, lane packets, Pass 1/Pass 2 transaction audit, four firing
shapes, run health, V60 source-backed enrichment, and product-surface hygiene.

Peter Bevelin's `Seeking Wisdom` should be treated as a potential enrichment
source in the same lineage, not as a replacement ontology. The book mostly
shares Munger's diagnosis of psychological misjudgment, but it adds a more
operational checklist discipline: evidence, incentives, alternatives,
downside, yardsticks, reversibility, state of mind, role reversal, and
postmortem learning.

The practical opportunity is therefore:

> Munger names the failure mode. The canonical model corpus supplies the
> corrective repertoire. Bevelin can make the tendency checks more operational:
> what must be verified, measured, challenged, reversed, or written down before
> a recommendation is trustworthy.

## What Is Already Strong

Current Lane 1 is already much closer to Bevelin than the old System B shape:

- Pass 1 and Pass 2 audit the conversation transaction, not the assistant in
  isolation.
- Four firing shapes are explicit: commission, omission, uncritical
  acceptance, and missed challenge.
- Pass 2 already treats skipped denominators, dependencies, reversal
  conditions, pilots, and stop rules as valid omission evidence.
- The subpattern catalog already contains many Bevelin-like operational
  checks: missing denominator, missing reversal condition, option-set collapse,
  counterargument-window skipped, reward-window over governance, absolute
  standard hidden by contrast, messenger punished for bad news, and
  lollapalooza firebreaks.
- V60 already has affordance records for evidence gates, optionality,
  premortem, switching-costs, WYSIATI, incentives, statistical discipline,
  falsifiability, opportunity cost, and related records.

This means Bevelin should not be used as prompt bulk. The engine already has
many concepts. The gap is selection, materiality, and operational sharpness.

## Bevelin's Distinct Contribution

Bevelin overlaps heavily with Munger's tendency list, but the useful addition
is the decision-audit grammar around those tendencies:

- What exactly is the issue?
- What is the measurable goal or avoided outcome?
- What cause or equation would make the claim true?
- What evidence supports it, and what evidence would disconfirm it?
- What representative evidence, track record, or reference class is available?
- What alternatives are live?
- What is the downside if wrong?
- What is the opportunity cost against the next best alternative?
- What yardstick measures progress or adequacy?
- Who benefits, who pays, who is responsible, and how are they measured?
- What state of mind or environment is influencing the actor?
- What system would be fair if roles were reversed?
- What would we want recorded for postmortem learning?

Those are not new tendencies. They are tendency detectors and corrective
pressure contracts.

## Tendency-Level Enrichment Opportunities

### Reward and Punishment

Current shape: strong subpatterns for compensation design, reward window over
governance, capture speed over readiness, and general incentive pressure.

Bevelin improvement: distinguish generic upside from actor self-interest. The
detector should ask whether the assistant failed to map who benefits, who pays,
who is measured, who is responsible for consequences, and what behavior the
system will actually reward after people adapt.

Candidate signal tags:

- `actor-self-interest-unmapped`
- `beneficiary-payer-split-hidden`
- `responsibility-without-consequence`
- `metric-rewards-wrong-behavior`

### Availability and Overoptimism

Current shape: availability has vivid proof substitution; overoptimism has
missing denominator and missing reversal condition.

Bevelin improvement: make representative evidence and track record a sharper
materiality test. A vivid user claim or assistant example should not fire merely
because it is vivid; it fires when the recommendation leans on it while skipping
the reference class, sample structure, failure frequency, or base-rate record
that the decision requires.

Candidate signal tags:

- `representative-evidence-missing`
- `track-record-absent`
- `sample-treated-as-population`
- `success-case-without-failures`

### Inconsistency, Deprival, and Status Quo

Current shape: status-quo protection, commitment before resolution, escalation
of prior design, switching-loss overweighting, takeaway pain dampening, and
target-loss escalation.

Bevelin improvement: add a recommitment test: if the actor had not already
spent time, money, reputation, or identity on this, would the assistant still
recommend starting today? If not, the assistant must name the exit/reversal
condition rather than treating continuation as the default.

Candidate signal tags:

- `start-again-today-test-missing`
- `future-value-recommitment-skipped`
- `loss-recovery-framed-as-obligation`
- `exit-condition-not-named`

### Contrast Misreaction

Current shape: this is already strong after the specificity correction:
contrast should not fire unless an absolute standard is hidden by a relative
frame.

Bevelin improvement: connect the hidden absolute standard to the goal and
yardstick. The challenge is not just "use an absolute measure." It is "what
yardstick would govern this decision if the nearby comparison disappeared?"

Candidate signal tags:

- `goal-yardstick-missing`
- `relative-frame-without-absolute-threshold`
- `cumulative-drift-not-measured`

### Reason-Respecting and Sensemaking

Current shape: narrative closes the why, correlation accepted as cause, shallow
first why stops inquiry.

Bevelin improvement: require the "equation for the goal" or causal mechanism.
The detector should look for reasons that sound explanatory but do not state
the mechanism, variables, evidence, or disconfirming condition.

Candidate signal tags:

- `reason-without-mechanism`
- `goal-equation-unstated`
- `causal-chain-not-tested`
- `coherent-story-without-falsifier`

### Social Proof and Authority

Current shape: borrowed consensus as proof, stress-amplified herd following,
inaction as consensus signal, contagious normalization; authority covers
prestige cues and authority overriding protocol.

Bevelin improvement: strengthen the "not wanting to stand out" and "bad news
carrier" shapes. In conversations, this often appears as the assistant treating
silence, lack of objection, expert presence, or peer adoption as if it had
already done the evidence work.

Candidate signal tags:

- `silence-treated-as-consensus`
- `dissent-channel-missing`
- `bad-news-carrier-incentive-hidden`
- `expert-status-without-independent-test`

### Stress and Doubt Avoidance

Current shape: deadline shortcutting, load-collapse omission, forced closure,
unknowns demoted, option-set collapse, counterargument window skipped.

Bevelin improvement: make state-of-mind a first-class context cue. Stress,
fatigue, confusion, excitement, embarrassment, fear, or relief can be why the
assistant should slow the decision. This should not become a generic "user is
emotional" detector; it matters only when the assistant commits while skipping
the stabilizing check the emotional state makes necessary.

Candidate signal tags:

- `state-of-mind-pressure-unchecked`
- `emotion-compresses-review`
- `forced-motion-before-deliberation`
- `relief-treated-as-readiness`

### Mere Association, Liking, and Disliking

Current shape: halo transfer, familiarity equals performance, price-quality
signal substitution, messenger punished for bad news, disliked presenter vetoes
valid option.

Bevelin improvement: Persian Messenger should be especially useful for missed
challenge detection. If the conversation contains an actor who has incentive to
avoid delivering bad news, and the assistant treats missing bad news as
reassurance, that is a material association/incentive compound.

Candidate signal tags:

- `missing-bad-news-as-reassurance`
- `messenger-incentive-unchecked`
- `pleasant-source-treated-as-evidence`
- `painful-message-discounted`

### Lollapalooza

Current shape: deterministic compound groups from Pass 2 findings; subpatterns
include undiagnosed compounding, cascade effects treated in isolation, and
missing structural firebreak.

Bevelin improvement: use his repeated warning that real misjudgments often
come from many factors together to build compound templates from archived
cases. Do this only after single-tendency detections are trustworthy.

Candidate compound templates:

- `reward + authority + social proof`: organizational silence around a powerful
  sponsor or customer.
- `deprival + inconsistency`: doubling down because exit admits loss.
- `availability + overoptimism`: vivid customer signal treated as growth proof.
- `stress + doubt avoidance`: urgent decision closes uncertainty before the
  necessary check.
- `association + incentive + simple denial`: bad-news channel disappears and
  comfort is treated as truth.

## Where To Change The System

### Do Not Add A New Ontology

Do not add Bevelin's 28 reasons as a parallel taxonomy. The Munger tendency
ontology remains the right Lane 1 detection skeleton. Bevelin should enrich
subpatterns, signal tags, source refs, affordance records, absence records, and
offline audits.

### Do Not Bulk-Ingest Into Prompts

The Pass 1 and Pass 2 prompts are already long and carefully tuned. Dumping
the checklist into them would raise generic-checklist overfire risk.

The safe prompt-level change, if any, is a small materiality reminder:

> For omission and missed-challenge cases, a skipped check is material only
> when it changes the decision pressure for this tendency. Common material
> checks include representative evidence, actor incentives, live alternatives,
> downside/reversibility, absolute yardstick, disconfirming condition, and
> state-of-mind pressure.

Even that should be A/B replayed before live promotion.

### Prefer Compiled Enrichment

The better first target is compiled substrate:

- Add Bevelin-backed source refs to existing subpatterns where he is directly
  strengthening the materiality test.
- Add signal tags where the current subpattern is right but too broad.
- Add V60 affordance/absence candidates for the checklist probes.
- Add an Observatory-only treatment audit before changing public output.

## Candidate V60/Compiled Affordance Families

These are not proposed records yet; they are extraction targets.

- `representative-evidence-gate`: require reference class, track record,
  sample structure, or failure distribution before treating evidence as
  decision weight.
- `actor-incentive-map`: require who benefits, who pays, who is measured, and
  who bears consequences.
- `downside-reversibility-gate`: require cost of being wrong, reversibility,
  and action if the bad branch appears.
- `absolute-yardstick-gate`: require the measure that governs the choice when
  relative comparison is removed.
- `alternative-opportunity-cost-gate`: require live alternatives and next-best
  opportunity before accepting the proposed path.
- `disconfirmation-prosecutor-gate`: require the evidence that would make the
  conclusion false.
- `state-of-mind-guard`: require a pause or externalized checklist when stress,
  fear, relief, urgency, or confusion is shaping commitment.
- `role-reversal-system-gate`: require the proposed rule/system to survive
  reversed roles and aligned incentives.
- `postmortem-record-gate`: require predecision reasons, expected signals, and
  later review criteria for learning.

## First PR Artifact

This PR implements that measurement-only shape as a deterministic local audit:

- `engine/system_b/bevelin_lane1_audit.py`
- `scripts/run_bevelin_lane1_audit.py`
- `tests/test_bevelin_lane1_audit.py`

It reads existing `result.json` artifacts and separates:

- LLM-created artifacts: Pass 1 triage, Pass 2 deep checks, companion, frame,
  and structural coverage boundary calls.
- embedding-created artifacts: Lane 1 Swiss-cheese candidate expansion and V60
  embedding-mode candidates where present.
- deterministic artifacts: triggered tendency set, routes, DeltaCard findings,
  V60 selection telemetry, V60 consideration ledger, coverage gaps, and run
  health.

For each Bevelin probe family it reports whether the probe was:

- `publicly_treated`;
- `privately_considered`;
- `lane1_pressure_only`;
- `candidate_only`;
- `missing`.

## Risks

- Generic checklist overfire: every answer can be accused of omitting some
  checklist item. Materiality must stay tied to a specific Munger tendency.
- Taxonomy drift: Bevelin's 28 reasons can tempt us into duplicating the
  tendency catalog. Avoid it.
- Prompt bloat: adding the checklist to Pass 1/Pass 2 could reduce precision.
- False confidence: more source material does not automatically mean better
  findings. Ledger and replay discipline matter more.
- Product bloat: the user-facing output must remain compact, dismissible, and
  action-changing.

## Recommendation

Treat `Seeking Wisdom` as a source-backed operationalization layer for Lane 1,
not a new lane and not a replacement canon.

The right enrichment sequence is:

1. Map Bevelin checklist probes to existing Munger tendencies and subpatterns.
2. Replay archived runs with an Observatory-only treatment audit.
3. Promote only the probes that improve action-delta and reviewer agreement.
4. Add source-backed affordance/absence records where the current V60 substrate
   lacks sharp operational pressure.
5. Only then consider tiny Pass 1/Pass 2 materiality wording changes.

The highest-leverage idea is simple:

> Lane 1 should not just ask "which tendency is present?" It should ask "which
> missing Bevelin-style test makes this tendency materially dangerous in this
> conversation?"
