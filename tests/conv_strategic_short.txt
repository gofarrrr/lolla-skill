[Turn 1] ASSISTANT:
At $2M ARR with 20% month-over-month growth, you'll hit $5M in roughly 5 months. But waiting until $5M is the wrong framing — the question is whether founder-led sales is currently the bottleneck to growth.

I'd recommend hiring now. Here's my reasoning: at 20% MoM, you're likely leaving deals on the table because founders can't follow up on all inbound. A strong VP of Sales takes 3-4 months to ramp, which means if you hire today, they're productive right when you hit the $5M mark. If you wait, you lose those 3-4 months of ramp time at higher volume.

The risk of hiring too early is burn — a VP of Sales at this stage costs $250-350K loaded. But at 20% MoM growth, the opportunity cost of delayed deals far exceeds the salary. Your founders' time is better spent on product and fundraising than running sales calls.

One caveat: make sure you hire someone who has built a sales team from scratch, not someone who managed an existing team at a larger company. The skillsets are completely different.
