# Tendency Catalog

The 25 cognitive tendencies that Lolla detects in strategic advice. Based on Charlie Munger's Psychology of Human Misjudgment, adapted for LLM-generated reasoning.

For each tendency: what it is, how it manifests in AI-generated advice, and the corrective mental models mapped to it with their activation contexts (the specific pattern that should trigger each corrective route).

## authority-misinfluence-tendency

**Description:** Following the instructions of an authority figure even when it causes harm or contradicts common sense.

**Core models (3):** authority-bias, power-dynamics, information-asymmetry

**Related dynamics (3):** social-proof, elasticity, signaling

**Corrective models (7):**

- `user-experience-research-methods` — forces the organization to place empirical evidence from actual user behavior above the rank or intuition of authority figures
- `psychological-safety` — creates the environment where subordinates can challenge authority without fear of retribution — when the answer defers to a senior figure's judgment and no structural mechanism exists for dissent or independent verification
- `intellectual-humility` — the disposition that prevents authority figures from treating their own expertise as infallible — when the answer accepts a leader's or expert's assertion without requiring them to acknowledge the limits of their knowledge or invite challenge
- `first-principles-thinking` — forces the rebuilding of the argument from foundational facts rather than accepting it on the authority's say-so — when the answer trusts a conclusion because of who said it rather than because the underlying logic has been independently verified
- `dialectical-reasoning` — forces the authority's thesis to face an antithesis, breaking the halo effect and demanding rigorous proof over rank
- `latticework-of-mental-models` — demands multidisciplinary fluency to cross-check and validate an authority's claims rather than blindly accepting their single-frame expertise
- `self-organization-and-emergent-order` — dissolves the illusion that complex systems require top-down authoritative dictate to function, proving that coherent outcomes can arise purely from decentralized local interactions

## availability-misweighing-tendency

**Description:** Overweighting evidence that is easily available, vivid, or recent.

**Core models (2):** survivorship-bias, gestalt-principles-of-perception

**Related dynamics (4):** usability-heuristics, narratives, black-swan-events, analogies-and-metaphors

**Corrective models (17):**

- `variation-and-selection` — structurally prevents the brain from seizing the first available idea by mandating a volume of alternatives
- `varied-practice-interleaving` — blocks the availability heuristic by deliberately rotating through diverse, non-obvious analytical lenses
- `base-rates` — forces the mind to prioritize robust statistical regularities "the outside view" over vivid, easily recalled anecdotes
- `law-of-large-numbers` — demands that conclusions be drawn from representative sample sizes rather than a single vivid case or small cluster of memorable events — when the answer generalizes from one dramatic example without checking whether the pattern holds across a broad population
- `statistics-concepts` — provides the quantitative toolkit to separate signal from noise — when the answer treats a salient data point as representative without testing it against proper statistical measures like variance, confidence intervals, or population parameters
- `scientific-method-evidence-testing` — forces the systematic testing of claims against controlled evidence rather than accepting them because the supporting anecdote is vivid or emotionally resonant — when the answer confuses a compelling narrative with verified causal proof
- `algorithmic-thinking` — replaces intuitive hot-cognition vividness with cold, analytical, step-by-step logic
- `critical-thinking` — the deliberate engagement of System 2 reasoning to question whether the most readily available evidence is actually the most relevant evidence — when the answer accepts information at face value because it is vivid or recent without subjecting it to structured skepticism
- `decomposition` — forces explicit mapping of the whole problem, revealing what data is missing rather than relying on vividly available facts
- `desirable-difficulties` — forces the analyst to resist the cognitive ease of the first available explanation and instead engage in the harder work of seeking disconfirming or less salient evidence — when the answer defaults to the most accessible interpretation because deeper analysis feels unnecessarily effortful
- `problem-framing-and-reframing` — forces the conscious testing of multiple frames, preventing the brain from lazily substituting the first available mental model for the complex actual reality
- `prioritization` — the structured defense that forces focus back to what is mathematically or structurally 'Important' [Eisenhower Matrix/MECE], neutralizing the brain's instinct to react to whatever is merely 'Urgent' or vividly available
- `reasoning-mode-router` — the conscious executive function that prevents the brain from lazily substituting a readily available framework for the correct, context-specific analytical method
- `second-order-thinking` — forces the mind past the vivid, immediate, first-order consequence to systematically map the less-obvious, delayed dominoes
- `statistical-discipline` — imposes mathematical rigor to counteract the brain's reliance on vivid, anecdotal noise
- `statistical-learning-theory` — forces the selection of explanatory models based on predictive validity across broad data rather than on how well they fit a single memorable case — when the answer overfits to vivid recent experience instead of testing whether the pattern generalizes to the reference class
- `step-back` — forces the cognitive shift from the fast, emotionally resonant System 1 reaction to the slow, analytical System 2 reasoning required to properly weigh evidence

## contrast-misreaction-tendency

**Description:** Relying on contrast rather than absolute measurement, especially when distorted peer sets, inflated comparables, or stepwise drift replace absolute judgment (e.g., the boiling frog).

**Core models (2):** anchoring, baseline-establishment

**Related dynamics (3):** delays, persuasion-principles, price-discrimination

**Corrective models (4):**

- `constraints` — imposes explicit, absolute boundaries on acceptable outcomes or scope so small leaks, tiny stepwise losses, and shifting baselines cannot quietly drag judgment toward disaster
- `data-science-reasoning-framework` — forces structured, hypothesis-driven analysis with absolute metrics instead of letting distorted peer sets or inflated comparables substitute for rigorous independent measurement
- `statistical-discipline` — imposes mathematical rigor on claims that rely on relative contrast — demanding base rates, proper denominators, and absolute thresholds rather than accepting that something looks acceptable only because it sits next to something worse
- `formal-reasoning` — exposes when the logical structure of an argument depends entirely on a comparison frame that, if removed, would collapse the conclusion — forces deductive validation of each premise on its own terms

## curiosity-tendency

**Description:** The innate drive to explore and learn; a powerful preventative force against other biases.

**Core models (2):** curiosity, lateral-thinking

**Related dynamics (3):** growth-mindset, intellectual-humility, flow

**Corrective models (4):**

- `scaffolding-educational` — turns curiosity into a durable anti-bias force by giving exploration enough staged educational structure that the learner can keep deepening understanding instead of stopping at confusion or surface practicality
- `first-principles-thinking` — channels curiosity into rigorous decomposition — when the answer accepts inherited assumptions or conventional framing without questioning whether the underlying premises are actually true
- `five-whys-method` — the operational engine of curiosity that prevents the inquiry from stopping at the first plausible explanation — when the answer diagnoses a problem or proposes a solution without tracing the causal chain to its structural root
- `meta-cognitive-reflection` — turns curiosity inward to audit the reasoning process itself — when the answer proceeds without pausing to ask whether the analytical frame being used is the right one or whether a blind spot is distorting the conclusion

## deprival-superreaction-tendency

**Description:** Loss aversion: extreme reaction to small losses, threatened losses, or the loss of a near-miss reward.

**Core models (2):** prospect-theory, regret-theory

**Related dynamics (2):** sunk-cost-fallacy, opportunity-cost

**Corrective models (7):**

- `decision-trees` — forces the chooser to predefine bid ceilings, fold points, and rescue-exit branches before near misses, takeaways, or get-even pressure can ratchet commitment upward
- `calculated-risk-taking` — forces systematic quantification of the actual risk rather than reacting to the emotional intensity of the threatened loss — when the answer escalates commitment or overpays to avoid a loss without calculating whether the rescue cost exceeds the value of what is being lost
- `expected-value` — forces rigorous probability-weighted evaluation of outcomes to override the disproportionate emotional weight the brain assigns to losses — when the answer treats a threatened loss as catastrophic without computing the actual expected cost across the range of scenarios
- `probabilistic-thinking` — replaces the binary panic of loss-or-no-loss with calibrated likelihood estimates — when the answer reacts to a near-miss or threatened deprivation as if the loss were certain rather than assigning it a realistic probability
- `emotional-intelligence` — provides self-management to prevent emotional hijacking when threatened with loss
- `endowment-effect` — explains why the pain of losing an owned item is irrationally higher than the pleasure of gaining it
- `law-of-large-numbers` — forces the mind to evaluate the expected value of a loss over a large sample, mitigating the irrational, immediate panic of a singular deprival event

## disliking-hating-tendency

**Description:** Ignoring virtues, distorting facts, and reciprocating extreme hostility.

**Core models (2):** causal-attribution-resistance, rationalization

**Related dynamics (2):** multicultural-team-dynamics, red-queen-effect

**Corrective models (14):**

- `non-violent-communication` — forces the structural separation of objective observations from emotional judgments, preventing the reciprocal escalation of hostility
- `cross-cultural-communication-frameworks` — forces the communicator to repackage ideas for the receiver's cognitive context rather than dismissing them as incomprehensible — when the answer rejects a proposal or perspective because the source belongs to an out-group whose communication style triggers automatic distrust
- `empathy` — forces cognitive perspective-taking to understand the disliked party's actual position rather than a hostile caricature — when the answer distorts or ignores the legitimate concerns of an adversary because animosity has replaced the willingness to understand their real motivations
- `active-listening` — forces ego suppression and validates experience without necessarily agreeing
- `dialectical-reasoning` — forces you to articulate the opponent's view so well you could defend it—"Perspective Taking"
- `emotional-intelligence` — provides the self-awareness to recognize "hot cognition" and the self-management to override instinctual hostility
- `constructive-feedback-models` — bypasses personal identity and focuses purely on observable behavior and its impact, neutralizing interpersonal friction
- `game-theory-payoffs` — forces players to abstract away the 'hate' and coldly calculate the adversary's actual incentives and likely moves
- `goal-setting` — specifically 'Superordinate Goals', which force warring parties to align on a shared, necessary outcome, structurally overriding their mutual hatred
- `hanlons-razor` — directly short-circuits the attribution of evil intent to an adversary, removing the foundational fuel for reciprocating extreme hostility
- `internal-locus-of-control` — demands the individual assume at least 1% responsibility for any conflict, breaking the self-righteousness that fuels extreme hostility
- `international-negotiation-and-diplomacy-models` — structures the interaction to bypass personal animosity and focus purely on interdependent interests and durable settlements
- `multi-criteria-decision-analysis` — forces the explicit weighting of objective options, preventing irrational hatred of the presenter from vetoing a valid solution
- `nash-equilibrium` — forces warring parties to calculate the mathematically stable optimal response rather than acting out of reciprocal spite

## doubt-avoidance-tendency

**Description:** The rush to reach a quick decision — often preferring any conclusion over none — to eliminate the stress of uncertainty, puzzlement, or ambiguity.

**Core models (2):** anchoring, status-quo-bias

**Related dynamics (2):** cognitive-biases, false-precision-avoidance

**Corrective models (22):**

- `step-back` — forces delay before decision and a temporary "mask" of objectivity when puzzlement and stress are pushing the mind to remove doubt too quickly
- `probabilistic-thinking` — replaces the binary need for certainty with calibrated likelihood estimates — when the answer treats an uncertain situation as requiring a definitive yes-or-no answer rather than accepting a range of probabilities and making decisions accordingly
- `true-uncertainty-navigation` — provides structured frameworks for acting under genuine ambiguity without rushing to false certainty — when the answer collapses a complex, multi-outcome situation into a single premature conclusion because sitting with uncertainty feels intolerable
- `risk-vs-uncertainty` — forces the system to explicitly map 'unknown unknowns' and convert them rather than just rushing to any available decision
- `decision-trees` — forces the explicit mapping of choices, probabilities, and payoffs before committing — when the answer leaps to a conclusion without systematically evaluating the branching paths and their expected outcomes
- `agile-methodologies` — replaces the urge for a finalized blueprint with comfort in ambiguity and iterative testing
- `aleatory-epistemic-uncertainty-recognition` — formalizes what cannot be known to prevent rushing to false certainty
- `dialectical-reasoning` — forces the team to sit with tension and conflict before premature synthesis
- `divergent-vs-convergent-thinking` — forces the brain to resist premature convergence and dwell in the divergent state of doubt to generate better options
- `experimentation` — forces the systematic testing of hypotheses, delaying the brain's instinct to rush to a comforting but unverified conclusion
- `five-whys-method` — structurally delays the comforting closure of a quick decision until the root cause is systematically unearthed
- `goal-setting` — establishing a concrete 'SMART' target cuts through the paralyzing ambiguity of doubt
- `information-theory` — provides structural scaffolding and chunking to reduce the cognitive overwhelm that often precedes and triggers doubt-avoidance panic
- `iteration` — replaces the urge for a finalized, perfect blueprint with comfort in ambiguity and systematic, incremental testing
- `lateral-thinking` — structurally prolongs the divergent, uncomfortable phase of ideation to prevent the brain from snapping prematurely to the first adequate solution
- `markov-chains` — forces the mind to move through structured, discrete states of analysis rather than leaping to a comforting conclusion
- `optionality` — forces the structural generation of at least three viable options before deciding, directly neutralizing the brain's rush to eliminate uncertainty via the first adequate conclusion
- `scientific-method-evidence-testing` — replaces intuitive hot-cognition vividness with cold, analytical logic, preventing the rush to the first available conclusion
- `simplification` — strips away the paralyzing noise and ambiguity that triggers doubt-avoidance panic
- `statistical-discipline` — imposes formal, probabilistic rigor to prevent the rush to the first comfortable conclusion
- `statistical-learning-theory` — forces the selection of hypotheses based on predictive validity rather than intuitive comfort
- `decomposition` — forces the mind to evaluate each dimension independently before reaching a holistic conclusion, delaying the premature integration that doubt-avoidance craves — cf. Kahneman: "take the separate dimensions and really think about each dimension separately"

- `user-experience-research-methods` — the structured abductive methodology that forces experts to observe actual user behavior, explicitly disproving their own ego-driven assumptions
- `intellectual-humility` — the foundational disposition that one's current views may be wrong and that better answers may come from others — when the answer treats the decision-maker's expertise or track record as sufficient justification without acknowledging the limits of their knowledge or inviting challenge from those with different perspectives
- `peer-review-your-perspectives` — structurally forces the 'inside view' of the ego to submit to the 'outside view' of diverse, objective scrutiny, preventing the brilliant but flawed hero from operating in a vacuum
- `extreme-performance-evaluation` — forces the ego to push its ideas and assumptions to the breaking point under adversarial conditions — when the answer assumes a plan or capability will perform well without stress-testing it against worst-case scenarios that would expose the gap between self-assessed and actual competence
- `active-listening` — overcoming the "Conversational Narcissist Trap"
- `authenticity` — radical truth demands facing weaknesses to counteract the inside view
- `blooms-taxonomy` — forces the realization that one cannot validly "Evaluate" without having done the foundational work to "Understand" and "Analyze"
- `brainstorming` — enforcing the "kill your babies" rule decouples ego from ideas
- `circle-of-competence` — the ultimate structural boundary against self-regard, demanding the intellectual humility to map the exact perimeter where your genuine advantage ends
- `cognitive-gaps-assessment` — actively dismantled the "Illusion of Explanatory Depth" by forcing experts to explicitly document their assumptions and hunt for blind spots
- `comparative-advantage` — acts as a guardrail against ego by forcing honest assessment of where one is weak, demanding delegation to those actually suited for the task
- `confidence-calibration` — shatters the 'Illusion of Knowing' by forcing experts to place exact probability bounds on their predictions, exposing unwarranted, ego-driven certainty
- `constructive-feedback-models` — institutionalizing "accurate criticism" to structurally dismantle the ego's 'inside view'
- `cross-cultural-communication-frameworks` — direct antidote to the "Curse of Knowledge" forcing the ego to subordinate its own "genius" to the cognitive needs of the receiver
- `cultural-intelligence` — forces the ego to recognize its own "inside view" and formally map the subjective, internal realities of others
- `deliberate-practice` — systematically zeroes in on sub-skills and weaknesses, overriding the ego's desire to comfortably display only strengths
- `feynman-technique` — shatters the 'Illusion of Explanatory Depth' by forcing the ego to translate its assumed genius into plain English, exposing what it actually doesn't know
- `generation-effect` — shatters the 'Illusion of Explanatory Depth' by forcing the learner to actively pull the idea out of their own head, breaking the ego-driven illusion that they "know" it just because they recognized the words
- `iteration` — demands 'killing your babies' and abandoning ego-invested ideas when evidence suggests failure
- `jobs-to-be-done` — forces the ego to accept that the customer does not care about the creator's 'genius' features, only the outcome
- `johari-window` — provides the formal grid for identifying the 'blind spot' pane—the exact area where Excessive Self-Regard hides flaws from the individual that are obvious to everyone else
- `latticework-of-mental-models` — forces humility by demanding the expert admit their single discipline is insufficient, requiring them to borrow tools from others
- `mental-models-of-reality` — forces humility by demanding the expert admit their single discipline is insufficient, requiring them to constantly test their internal 'map' against the objective 'territory'
- `meta-cognitive-reflection` — turns the camera lens inward to explicitly audit the self's own thinking, breaking the blind spot of unexamined ego

**Related dynamics (4):** path-dependence, lock-in, switching-costs, signaling

**Corrective models (20):**

- `tradition-vs-innovation-balance` — the explicit framework that forces an organization to systematically break status-quo habits by institutionalizing measured, incongruent novelty
- `variation-and-selection` — forces structured divergence before any commitment can lock in
- `creative-destruction` — the active willingness to destroy existing structures, assumptions, and prior commitments when evidence shows they are no longer fit — when the answer protects an existing approach or investment because dismantling it feels like admitting the original decision was wrong
- `reframing-perspective` — forces the conscious adoption of alternative viewpoints to escape the single frame that the prior commitment has locked in — when the answer evaluates a situation exclusively through the lens of the original plan or strategy without testing whether a completely different frame would reveal better options
- `problem-framing-and-reframing` — forces breaking the first-conclusion bias by deliberately testing multiple, divergent cuts at the problem before synthesis
- `scientific-method-evidence-testing` — demands that the prior conclusion be treated as a hypothesis to be tested against new evidence rather than a settled truth to be defended — when the answer treats the existing approach as given and only looks for confirming evidence instead of rigorously testing whether it still holds
- `falsifiability` — forces breaking the first-conclusion bias by systematically trying to falsify the preferred conclusion instead of confirming it
- `elasticity` — forces stretching across fixed functional boundaries to override habitual ruts
- `first-principles-thinking` — forces the destruction of the inherited premise, compelling the builder to start from elemental facts instead of optimizing a stale template
- `generation-effect` — getting individuals to actively formulate a new conclusion themselves effectively overrides the old mental habit loop much stronger than passive instruction
- `growth-mindset` — actively seeks to break existing paradigms and re-write models of reality, fighting the brain's reluctance to change previous conclusions
- `internal-locus-of-control` — provides the agency needed to actively dismantle expired beliefs instead of passively accepting the mental status quo
- `iteration` — forces the systemic breaking of previous conclusions through hypothesis testing, directly contrary to the brain's desire for a static status quo
- `lateral-thinking` — forces the mind to abandon the comfortable, habitual linear path and deliberately generate novel, non-obvious alternatives
- `latticework-of-mental-models` — forces the brain out of its single comfortable 'status quo' frame by demanding multiple, multidisciplinary 'cuts' at the problem
- `mental-models-of-reality` — the foundational baseline: adopting multiple models inherently breaks the mind's tendency to defend a single, static paradigm
- `natural-selection-analogy` — demands the continuous destruction of unfit ideas and habits, structurally forbidding adherence to a static status quo
- `regulatory-horizon-scanning` — actively fights the 'status quo bias' of waiting for certainty; it forces organizations to structurally pivot their models and roadmaps based on weak signals of future change rather than clinging to locked-in inherited assumptions
- `six-thinking-hats` — structurally forces the mind out of its default, comfortable status-quo perspective by mandating the temporary adoption of alternative, unnatural cognitive frames
- `step-back` — the deliberate pause that forces a shift from the automatic defense of a previous conclusion to a systemic re-evaluation of its current validity

- `psychological-safety` — to cure Persian Messenger
- `law-of-large-numbers` — forces the evaluation of a proposition based on broad, statistical base-rates rather than being anchored by a single, vivid association

## overoptimism-tendency

**Related dynamics (5):** conjunction-fallacy, anchoring, bottlenecks, black-swan-events, emergence

**Corrective models (22):**

- `zone-of-development` — forces realistic, incremental progression rather than the optimistic assumption of heroic leaps
- `trade-offs` — forces the optimist to explicitly name the hidden cost, displaced benefit, or downside of their preferred "sunny case" outcome
- `true-uncertainty-navigation` — disciplines the optimist by demanding scenario analysis and treating decisions as 'bets' against unknown unknowns, rather than single-point forecasts
- `base-rates` — checking the reference class of historical failures instead of relying on the optimistic "inside view" narrative of the specific project
- `margin-of-safety` — demands objective historical error rates, not internal confidence
- `monte-carlo-methods` — forces the optimist to model the full distribution of possible outcomes rather than anchoring on a single sunny-case base estimate — when the answer treats an uncertain projection as a point forecast without examining the range, variance, or tail risks
- `premortem` — forces the team to assume the plan has already failed and work backward to identify the causes — when the answer commits to a course of action without systematically imagining the specific ways it could go wrong
- `agile-methodologies` — replaces massive up-front "planning fallacy" bets with small, testable increments
- `aleatory-epistemic-uncertainty-recognition` — forces distribution-of-outcomes thinking over a singular base-case
- `antifragility` — replaces the optimistic prediction of avoiding shocks with a system designed to benefit from them
- `calculated-risk-taking` — forces expected-value mathematics and reference class forecasting to override the emotional "inside view"
- `butterfly-effect` — forces the optimist to systematically trace the second- and third-order ramifications instead of assuming a clean, linear win
- `chain-of-verification` — structurally assaults the "Conjunctive Events Bias" where optimists blindly assume every sequential step of a complex plan will magically succeed
- `counterfactual-reasoning` — shatters the sunny-case baseline by explicitly modeling "anti-goals" and worst-case scenarios
- `expected-value` — forces rigorous mathematical evaluation of probability-weighted outcomes to override wishful 'inside view' narratives
- `extreme-performance-evaluation` — forces the optimist to explicitly design and simulate the worst-case scenario to see if it is survivable
- `goal-setting` — strict, measurable 'SMART' goals force rosy, abstract predictions to hit hard mathematical and temporal reality
- `law-of-large-numbers` — the ultimate mathematical defense against overoptimism, demanding that estimates be grounded in the historical base-rate of similar projects rather than wishful thinking
- `mental-simulation` — forces the optimist to explicitly mentally rehearse the worst-case scenario and tail risks rather than just the sunny-case baseline
- `redundancy` — the structural engineering of backup systems that formally acknowledges the high probability of component failure in complex plans, neutralizing blind optimism
- `risk-assessment` — demands the systematic anticipation of downside failure and "unknown unknowns," mitigating unwarranted optimism
- `risk-vs-uncertainty` — forces the translation of false, optimistic confidence into structured, measurable risk thresholds
